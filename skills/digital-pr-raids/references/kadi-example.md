# Worked Example — Kadi Luggage "Hidden Airline Fees"

A complete RAIDS campaign to model new ones on. This data-led campaign earned coverage across Australian Traveller, Nine, Yahoo Finance, The West, The New Daily and more (DR 39–92).

## Recon — the idea
- **Client:** Kadi Luggage (luggage brand) — kadiluggage.com
- **Idea:** "The Hidden Cost of Flying: which airlines charge the most hidden fees in Australia."
- **Why it works:** everyone flies, everyone hates surprise checkout fees, it's AU-first, and it ties naturally back to a luggage brand (pack light, avoid baggage fees).
- **Headline:** "Revealed: The Airlines Charging the Most Hidden Fees in Australia."
- **Smaller ideas:** Most Expensive Add-Ons in the Sky; Budget Airlines That Aren't Really Budget; Airline Transparency Index.

## Analyze — the data
Analysed a 6,000–8,000km route, 7-day round trip, standardising the add-ons (20kg checked + 7kg cabin + seat selection) and measuring the % increase from initial to final price across the 10 most popular AU airlines.

**Key findings**
- Malaysia Airlines: highest, up to **66%** more than the base fare.
- Virgin Australia, Qatar Airways, Air New Zealand: **no** additional charges.
- Scoot and Jetstar: **30%+** more for seat selection + checked baggage.
- Cathay Pacific: highest seat-selection fee at 11.96%.

The ranked table (airline × baggage % × seat % × total %) is the citable asset.

## Infiltrate — the media list
20–30 travel, national-news and consumer-finance journalists who'd covered airline pricing in the last 12 months — Traveller, Escape, 9News, Finder, Canstar, etc. Emails verified before sending.

## Deploy — the pitch
- **Subject:** "'Hidden' airline charges: Aussies being charged up to 66% more."
- **Body:** one-line hook → 3 bullet findings → dataset link → offer of an expert interview.
- **Press release:** headline, findings, ranked data table, a named co-founder quote, methodology, About Kadi, and an editor's note asking for a linked credit to kadiluggage.com.
- **Follow-up** after 5–7 days offering an exclusive local angle.

## Score — the results
Every placement logged in the coverage tracker: campaign type, date, link type (Clean vs Mention), DR, what it linked to, the client link, the article URL, and the anchor text — so total links, mentions and average DR can be reported per campaign.

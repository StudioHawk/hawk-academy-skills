# The RAIDS Protocol — full detail

AI search (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) surfaces brands that people talk about online. Digital PR earns those mentions and the high-authority backlinks that come with them. RAIDS is the repeatable system: **Recon → Analyze → Infiltrate → Deploy → Score.** Run one campaign per quarter; each adds mentions, visibility and trust.

---

## R — Recon (Ideation)
**Goal:** a story idea people actually care about.

**Checklist**
- Write down the main questions the audience asks.
- Scan Google News, Google Trends, Reddit, TikTok and blogs for what's hot.
- Brainstorm 3–5 possible headlines.

**Headline formulas**
- "The most [X] in [place/industry]"
- "Best and worst [X]"
- "[X] hidden cost revealed"
- "Revealed: The [X] charging the most [Y] in [place]"

**Ideation sheet fields** (the deliverable): Client, URL, Date, Reason, Main campaign idea/title, Has it been done before?, Headline + alternates, How we'll collect the data, Time/budget check, Limitations, Regional hooks, Which outlets could run it, Dream publications, Why would anyone care?, Markets, plus 2–3 smaller/reactive ideas.

A strong idea is: data-led, novel-or-fresh-angle, regionally hookable, and obviously interesting to a named set of outlets.

---

## A — Analyze (Data & Story)
**Goal:** back the idea with real facts.

**Checklist**
- Find data: government reports, industry stats, surveys, Google Trends, public listings.
- Use AI to spot patterns in the data.
- Turn findings into: one clear headline, 3–5 bullet points, and a simple chart or table.

**Example** — Data: 100,000 flights. Story: Sydney 12–3pm flights leave 25 mins early; Melbourne has the worst delays. Headline: "Revealed: the 3-hour window that beats flight delays in Australia."

The dataset becomes a shareable asset (xlsx) journalists can cite — always offer it as a link in the pitch.

---

## I — Infiltrate (Media List)
**Goal:** find the right reporters.

**Checklist**
- Keep the list short and targeted: 20–30 names.
- Use Muck Rack (or equivalent) to search by topic.
- Check each journalist has written about your subject in the last 12 months.
- Use Hunter.io to find and verify emails.

**Columns:** Outlet | Journalist Name | Email Address | Article URL | Notes.

Relevance beats volume. One journalist who covered "airline fees" last month is worth fifty generic travel-desk addresses.

---

## D — Deploy (Pitch & Outreach)
**Goal:** send the story so it's easy to publish.

**Checklist**
- Strong subject line (lead with the number/finding).
- Keep the email short: 2-sentence intro + 3 bullet facts + dataset link.
- Add press notes (methodology, quote, about, credit request) at the bottom.
- Follow up ONCE after 5–7 days, offering an exclusive local angle.

**Pitch structure**
1. Subject line — the hooky stat.
2. One-line "why this matters" intro.
3. 3 bullet key findings.
4. Dataset link + offer of extras/interview.
5. Press release below: headline, findings, data table, named quote, — END —, methodology, about, editor's note requesting a linked credit.

---

## S — Score (Results & Tracking)
**Goal:** record proof of what worked.

**Checklist**
- Save every coverage link as it lands.
- Log it in the coverage tracker.

**Tracker columns:** Type Of Campaign | Campaign Name | Date Published | Link Type (Clean link / Mention) | Domain Rating (DR) | Linked to (Homepage/category) | Client Link | Link to Article | Anchor Text.

Track DR and link-vs-mention so you can report total links, total mentions, and average DR per campaign. "Clean" = a real backlink; "Mention" = brand named without a link (still useful for AI visibility — chase a link where possible).

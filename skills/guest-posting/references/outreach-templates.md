# Guest-post outreach templates

Keep every pitch short, personalised, and value-first. Lead with *their* site, offer a
genuinely useful idea, make one ask. Follow up **once** after 5–7 days.

## Template 1 — The pitch (cold)
> **Subject:** Guest post idea for [Site] — [angle in 4–5 words]
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm [Your name] from [Business] in [Suburb]. I really rate your piece on **[their recent article]** — [one specific, genuine detail].
>
> I'd love to write a guest post for [Site] on **[topic]**, built around **[your unique asset — original data / a client-results story / a free tool]**. A working angle:
> - **[Headline idea]** — [one line on what readers get]
>
> It'd be original, exclusive to you, and ready in [X days]. Happy to send an outline first.
>
> Cheers,
> [Name] · [website]

## Template 2 — The follow-up (day 5–7)
> **Subject:** Re: Guest post idea for [Site]
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> Just floating this back up — still keen to write **[topic]** for [Site], and happy to tailor the angle to whatever your readers need most right now.
>
> No worries if it's not a fit. Thanks!
> [Name]

## Template 3 — Link insertion (when a guest post is overkill)
> **Subject:** Quick addition for your [topic] page
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> Your guide on **[their URL]** is a great resource. We just published **[your asset]** which covers [specific value] — might be a useful addition for your readers.
>
> Either way, keep up the great work.
> [Name] · [website]

## Do / Don't
- **Do** personalise the first line (name a real article). **Don't** mass-blast.
- **Do** offer original/exclusive content. **Don't** offer spun or AI-filler posts.
- **Do** one site, one ask, one follow-up. **Don't** chase more than twice.
- **Do** request a contextual link to a relevant page (not just the homepage) with natural anchor text.
- Australian English throughout.
